01 The opportunity

What East Hartford Public Schools is actually buying.

EHPS wants an AI mentor for every employee — certified and classified — that gives on-demand, job-embedded guidance. The RFP is emphatic about the one thing that matters most:

★ The line that defines the bid
"Unlike general-purpose AI tools, the selected platform must integrate directly with EHPS-specific policies, curriculum, instructional frameworks, technology systems, and operational documents, ensuring that the guidance staff receive is accurate, relevant, and consistent with how the district actually operates."

That sentence is a rejection of the ChatGPT-wrapper. It asks for an AI that answers from East Hartford's own reality — which is precisely what the Context Graph index tier exists to provide.

02 The joint solution

One mentor experience, two specialised tiers.

03 Who does what

A clean division that puts each partner on its strength.

PolarTie — the platform

The experience staff touch every day.

  • Conversational AI mentor agents (voice, avatar, multilingual, real-time translation)
  • Interactive role-play & practice; escalation to humans with full context
  • Apps (web/mobile/kiosk), branding, scheduling & nudges delivery
  • Analytics dashboards, transcripts/summaries, role-based access control
  • AWS hosting, encryption, tenant data ownership

Context Graph — the index tier our scope

The knowledge foundation that makes answers true.

  • Secure ingestion of EHPS policies, frameworks, curriculum, tool docs
  • Grounded retrieval that returns source citations, not guesses
  • Decision lineage & temporal validity — which policy is in force now
  • Compliance rules engine — enforce/flag district policy, with an audit trail
  • Per-tenant isolation; EHPS data never trains shared models
The strategic point
The index tier owns all three scored criteria (data isolation, K-12 focus, localized alignment) plus compliance — the hardest, most-differentiating part of the RFP. PolarTie owns the delivery surface. Neither partner is doing the other's job.

04 Our role: the index tier, in detail

What happens between an EHPS document and a grounded, compliant answer.

05 What makes it district-aligned

The contextual quadruple — facts plus the decision behind them.

A flat knowledge base stores what a policy says. Our index stores the decision: who adopted it, why, under which policy, and for how long. That is what lets the mentor be accurate and current rather than generic.

06 Integration: MCP today, code-level for depth

Joint submission, shared code — two integration depths, chosen per need.

① MCP standard

PolarTie already supports "External Knowledge via MCP — live AI connection to third-party or proprietary knowledge." Context Graph already ships an MCP server. The connection is a capability both products have today — not custom glue.

  • Fast to stand up; clean tier boundary
  • PolarTie agents call CG tools for grounded answers, citations, precedent & compliance checks
  • Ideal for the pilot and the demo

② Code-level deep

As a joint submission with shared code, the index tier can be embedded directly in PolarTie's stack — tighter latency, shared data model, and features that span both tiers (e.g. compliance gating inside the agent loop).

  • Lowest latency; richest feature coupling
  • Shared isolation & auth model
  • Path for roadmap features (role/clearance security, calendar nudges)
Why flexibility matters to EHPS
We can start on MCP for a fast, low-risk pilot and deepen to code-level integration without re-architecting — a de-risked path the district can see from day one.

07 The three scored criteria — all ours

EHPS will evaluate the Strategic Fit on these three. Each is a property of the index tier.

Data isolation

Per-tenant partitioning across graph, vector, and KV stores. EHPS data answers only EHPS questions and never trains a shared model. Dedicated-tenant deployment available.

K-12 focus

Policy binders, frameworks, certified/classified roles, and the school calendar are native concepts. Reasons about which policy is in force, not just what a document says.

Localized model alignment

Alignment by grounding, not fine-tuning a generic model — every answer is built from EHPS's own documents with citations, kept current by lineage + continuous ingestion.

08 Examples

What a staff member actually experiences — and what the index tier does underneath. (Illustrative.)

① Ask AI — grounded, with a citation

New paraeducator
Do I need to complete any training before I start working with students?
AI Mentor (PolarTie) · grounded by Context Graph
Yes. As a classified paraeducator you must complete Mandated Reporter and Bloodborne Pathogens / Safety training before your first day, and they renew annually. Your Mandated Reporter cert is due by Sep 1. Want me to open the module?
◆ Source: EHPS Policy 4148 — adopted Aug 2024 (BoE)

Underneath: the index tier retrieved the requirement edge, returned the exact policy as a citation, and used the validity window to compute the deadline — no guessing.

② Compliance gate — the mentor stays inside policy

When guidance could touch a policy boundary, the rules engine checks it and attaches an auditable reason. Example audit record returned to the platform:

{
  "outcome": "FLAG",
  "rule": "discount? no — restricted-guidance: discipline",
  "matched_concept": "STUDENT_DISCIPLINE", "score": 0.91, "threshold": 0.6,
  "reason": "Disciplinary guidance must route to an administrator — see Policy 5114",
  "model_id": "minishlab/potion-retrieval-32M"
}

The agent escalates to a human (PolarTie) instead of improvising — accurate and defensible, with the matched concept · score · threshold on record.

③ Contextual nudge — aligned to the calendar

Seasonal nudge
📅 Evaluation season opens Monday. Here's the EHPS instructional framework rubric and your self-reflection timeline — the goal-setting form is due Oct 15.
◆ Source: Teacher Evaluation Plan §2 · validity: 2026–27 cycle

Driven by validity windows + rules in the index tier; delivered by PolarTie at the right moment.
